I am a Colombian astronomer investigating Galaxies and Quasars in the Epoch of Reionization. I am currently doing my second year as a doctoral student at the International Max Planck Research School in Heidelberg, Germany (IMPRS-HD) program.

You can contact me HERE.

One of my passions is bringing Astronomy to the Colombian community and showcasing the work Colombian astronomers do internationally. I am part of the committee of the "Red de Estudiantes Colombianos de Astronomía" (RECA) where we build a lot of programs for Colombian students in schools and universities wanting to study and do research in Astronomy.

About Me

My journey thorugh astronomy started when I was a little girl watching Cosmos and stargazing with my telescope. However, astronomers like Carl Sagan seemed to be a myth to me growing up as I had never heard of anyone in Colombia studying the universe. When I turned 16, I left my home and went to the U.S. to prepare my university applications and finally be able to study Astronomy. A year later, I was accepted at The University of Texas at Austin where I obtained a B.S. in Astronomy and a B.S. in Phyisics. So, now I am finally an astronomer and I love it! I am still working hard and doing a lot of research at The Max Planck Institute for Astronomy (MPIA) to get my PhD.

Research Projects

I have lead different projects and telescope proposals focused on studying galaxies and quasars in the high-redshift universe (z ≳ 6), or when the Universe was less than a Billion years old.

I have worked with different telescopes on Earth and in space covering the optical/near-IR and radio wavelengths. Here is a quick view of the JWST Cy1 Proposal that I Co-PI with Dr. Micaela Bagley to confirm and study the ionization potential of a set of z ~ 9 galaxies.
